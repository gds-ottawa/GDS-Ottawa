⚠️ 5 Clear Signs Your Cable is Broken

  • Loose or Hanging Cables: One or both cables hanging loose, lying on the floor, or completely detached
  • Crooked Door: Door is tilted or uneven when opening—only lifts on one side
  • Loud Snapping Sound: You hear a loud snap or bang from the garage
  • Frayed or Rusted Cables: Visible wear, rust, or completely snapped cable
  • Door Won't Open: Door is stuck or only opens partially, cables wrapped around drums

⚠️ DANGER: Never Operate Door with Broken Cable

Do NOT use your garage door (manually or with opener) if a cable is broken or off the drum. The remaining cable will be overloaded and can fail catastrophically, causing the 300-400 lb door to crash down. This can cause serious injury, crush vehicles, or destroy the door. Call us immediately—we offer same-day emergency service.

Why Do Garage Door Cables Break?

1. Normal Wear and Fraying

After 10,000+ lifting cycles (5-7 years of typical use), cable strands fray and weaken. Each use puts stress on the steel fibers, eventually causing failure.

2. Rust and Corrosion

Ottawa winters with road salt, moisture, and humidity cause cables to rust. Rusty cables become brittle and snap suddenly without warning.

3. Broken Spring Impact

When a spring breaks, it whips the cable or causes a sudden shock load. This often causes cables to snap or jump off the drums.

4. Bottom Bracket Failure

Rusty or bent bottom brackets let cables slip off. Common on 1990s-2010s builder-grade doors with thin metal brackets.

💡 Prevention Tip: Annual inspections can catch cable wear before failure. We check cables, brackets, and drums during spring maintenance visits.

Cable Problem or Spring Problem? How to Tell—and When to Call

Ottawa homeowners often wonder whether they need cable repair or spring repair. Both can leave your door stuck or crooked. Here’s a quick guide:

Cable issues: You see a cable hanging loose, lying on the floor, or wrapped around the drum. The door may tilt to one side when opening, or one side lifts while the other doesn’t. A loud snap may have occurred. Cable and bottom bracket replacement typically fixes this—we do it same day across Ottawa.

Spring issues: The door is very heavy to lift manually (or won’t stay open), and you may see a gap in the torsion spring or a broken extension spring. Springs and cables often fail around the same time; we routinely replace both cables and springs when both are worn. If you’re unsure, call 343-777-8893 and describe what you see—we’ll advise whether you need cable repair, spring repair, or both, and give you a price range over the phone.

💡 Should You Replace Both Cables?

Yes — we always recommend replacing both cables together, even if only one broke. Here's why:

  • Even Wear: If one cable broke, the other is likely worn to the same degree. It will probably fail within weeks or months.
  • Single Service Call: Replacing both now saves you a second $150+ emergency call when the other cable inevitably breaks.
  • Balanced Operation: New cables on both sides ensure even tension and smooth door movement. Mismatched cables cause imbalance.
  • Safety: A door with one old cable and one new cable is still a safety hazard. The old cable could snap under load.
  • Cost Savings: Two cables replaced together: $150-$250 total. Two separate calls: $300-$500 total (2× service fees).

Our Standard: We replace both cables in 99% of cases. It's the right thing to do for your safety and your wallet.

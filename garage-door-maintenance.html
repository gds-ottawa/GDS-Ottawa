Complete 21-Point Inspection & Service

🔍

Visual Inspection

Check springs, cables, tracks, rollers, hinges, brackets, sensors, and weather stripping for wear, damage, or rust.

🛢️

Complete Lubrication

Apply high-quality lubricant to all moving parts: rollers, hinges, tracks, springs, and opener chain/belt.

🔧

Hardware Tightening

Tighten all loose bolts, screws, and brackets. Ottawa's cold winters cause hardware to loosen over time.

⚖️

Door Balance Test

Test balance by manually lifting door halfway. Adjust spring tension if door doesn't stay in place.

📏

Track Alignment

Check tracks for proper alignment and spacing. Misaligned tracks cause binding and premature wear.

🛡️

Safety Sensor Test

Test sensors with obstruction. Clean lenses, adjust alignment, verify auto-reverse works properly.

⚙️

Opener Force Check

Test opener force and limit settings. Adjust if door reverses too easily or doesn't stop correctly.

❄️

Weather Stripping

Inspect bottom seal and side stripping. Replace if cracked to keep Ottawa's cold air and snow out.

✅

Final Testing

Test operation multiple times, check for smooth movement, listen for noises, verify all safety features work.

📅 How Often Should You Schedule Maintenance?

General Recommendation: Every 1-2 Years

For most residential garage doors in Ottawa, we recommend professional maintenance every 1-2 years. However, the ideal frequency depends on your usage:

📆 Annual Maintenance (Every Year)

Recommended if:
• You use door 10+ times per day
• You have an attached garage
• Your door is 10+ years old
• You want maximum reliability
• You park expensive vehicles inside

📅 Every 2 Years

Acceptable if:
• You use door 3-6 times per day
• Door is relatively new (under 5 years)
• You have a detached garage
• No unusual noises or problems
• Budget-conscious schedule

⚠️ Immediate Service Needed

Call us now if:
• Door makes grinding/squealing noises
• Door is off-balance or crooked
• Door jerks or stops mid-cycle
• Haven't had service in 3+ years
• Springs or cables show rust/wear

🥶 Ottawa Winters = More Wear

Ottawa's harsh winters (-20°C to -30°C) accelerate wear and tear:

  • Metal Fatigue: Springs and cables become brittle in extreme cold
  • Lubricant Breakdown: Grease thickens or dries out faster
  • Hardware Loosening: Freeze-thaw cycles loosen bolts and brackets
  • Weather Seal Damage: Rubber becomes brittle and cracks

Bottom Line: Ottawa doors need maintenance more frequently than doors in milder climates. Annual service is ideal for maximum reliability.

What Our Ottawa Maintenance Visit Catches Before It Becomes a Repair

During a garage door tune-up, our technicians look for early signs of wear that often lead to bigger problems. Catching them early can save you hundreds of dollars and avoid same-day emergency calls.

Springs & cables: Fraying cables, rust, or weak spring tension are flagged so you can plan a spring or cable replacement before a snap leaves your door stuck. We lubricate and test balance; if something is near the end of its life, we’ll say so and quote a repair.

Opener & safety: We check force and limit settings, test safety sensors, and lubricate the drive. Noisy or inconsistent operation can often be fixed during the maintenance visit; if the opener is failing, we’ll recommend repair or replacement with clear pricing.

Tracks, rollers & hardware: Loose bolts, misaligned tracks, and worn rollers cause binding and noise. Tightening and lubrication during maintenance often restore smooth operation and extend the life of the door. We serve all Ottawa areas—call 343-777-8893 to schedule.

How often should I schedule garage door maintenance? We recommend once per year, ideally in the fall before Ottawa’s winter.

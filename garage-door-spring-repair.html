⚠️ 7 Clear Signs Your Spring is Broken

  • Loud Bang: You hear a gunshot-like sound from the garage (spring breaking under 200-300 lbs of tension)
  • Heavy Door: Door is extremely heavy to lift manually—150-450 lbs without working springs
  • Won't Open: Door only opens 6-12 inches then stops (opener can't handle full weight)
  • Visible Gap: You see a 2-3 inch gap in the torsion spring above the door
  • Crooked Door: Door hangs at an angle or looks uneven when opening
  • Loose Cables: Cables hang down on one or both sides of the door
  • Slams Shut: Door closes very fast or slams down hard (no spring counterbalance)

⚠️ DANGER: Do Not Attempt DIY Spring Replacement

Garage door springs are under 200-300 lbs of extreme tension. One wrong move can cause serious injury, broken bones, or death. Professional replacement takes 30-60 minutes and costs $250-$350—not worth risking your life. Always call a certified technician.

❄️ Why Ottawa Winters Accelerate Spring Wear

Ottawa's harsh winters are brutal on garage door springs. Here's why springs fail faster in our climate:

Metal Contraction in Extreme Cold

When temperatures drop to -20°C to -30°C, steel springs contract and become brittle, increasing stress on the coils. Each freeze-thaw cycle weakens the metal's molecular structure.

Increased Cycle Count

In winter, most Ottawa homeowners use their garage door 2-3 times MORE than in summer because no one wants to be outside. More usage = faster spring fatigue.

Ice and Moisture Buildup

Snow and ice can add 50-100 lbs of extra weight. Springs work harder, causing premature wear. Moisture also accelerates rust formation.

📊 Ottawa Statistics: We see 40% more spring failures in January-February compared to summer. Springs in Ottawa typically last 5-7 years vs. 10-12 years in milder climates.

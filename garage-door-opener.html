🚨 7 Clear Signs Your Opener Needs Repair

  • Motor Hums But Door Doesn't Move: Usually means broken gears inside the opener—most common repair ($220-$250)
  • Door Only Opens 6-12 Inches Then Stops: Limit switches need adjustment or logic board failure
  • Remote/Wall Button Does Nothing: Could be dead batteries, bad receiver, or logic board issue
  • Lights Blink Continuously: Indicates error codes—safety sensors or mechanical problem
  • Grinding, Squealing, or Clicking Noises: Worn gears, chain/belt issues, or motor bearing failure
  • Door Reverses Immediately: Safety sensor misalignment or force settings too sensitive
  • Intermittent Operation: Works sometimes, not others—usually electrical or remote control issue

💡 Can It Be Repaired or Do You Need a New Opener?

About 60-70% of opener problems can be fixed same-day: broken gears ($220-$250), bad logic boards ($150-$220), sensor issues ($150-$200). However, if your opener is 12-15+ years old, replacement usually makes more financial sense. Modern openers are quieter, safer, and have smart features.

🏠 Why Ottawa Homeowners Choose Belt-Drive

For attached garages in Ottawa, belt-drive is the smart choice. Here's why:

  • Whisper-Quiet Operation: No metal-on-metal noise. Perfect when bedroom is above garage. Won't wake family at 6 AM.
  • Longer Lifespan: 15-20+ years vs 10-15 for chain-drive. Better ROI over lifetime.
  • Smoother Operation: Less vibration means less wear on door panels, tracks, and house structure.
  • Ottawa Winter Performance: Works reliably in -30°C. Belt material stays flexible in extreme cold.
  • Property Value: Adds value to home. Buyers prefer quiet openers, especially in attached garages.

💡 Our Recommendation: For attached garages, spend the extra $100-$150 for belt-drive. It's worth it for the quiet operation and longer lifespan.

Garage Door Opener Repair vs. Replacement: When to Fix vs. Install New

Ottawa homeowners often ask whether to repair an existing opener or replace it. Here’s a quick guide:

Repair makes sense when: The opener is under 15 years old, the motor runs but the door doesn’t move (often a gear/sprocket issue), remotes or wall button don’t work (often a logic board or sensor), or one component has failed (e.g. circuit board, gear kit). We repair all brands—LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Genie, Craftsman—and most repairs are $150–$280 with same-day service in Ottawa.

Replacement makes sense when: The opener is 15+ years old and noisy or unreliable, you want a quieter belt-drive or smart WiFi opener, repeated repairs would cost more than a new unit, or the motor has burned out.

Garage Door Spring Replacement Ottawa: What to Expect

Broken garage door springs are one of the most common repair calls in Ottawa. Here’s what you need to know about torsion vs extension springs, why we usually replace both, and what to expect from a same-day repair.

Torsion vs Extension Springs

Torsion springs sit above the door opening on a metal bar. They’re under high tension and must be handled by a trained technician. Extension springs run along the sides of the door. Both eventually wear out (often after 7–15 years, or sooner in Ottawa’s freeze-thaw cycles). When one fails, the other is usually close behind — so we recommend replacing both to avoid a second visit and to keep the door balanced.

Why Replace Both?

Springs are matched to the weight of the door. Replacing only one can leave the door unbalanced and stress the opener and tracks. Replacing both adds relatively little cost and gives you years of reliable operation. We use 10,000-cycle rated springs and warranty our work.

Safety First

Do not attempt to repair or replace torsion springs yourself. The stored energy can cause serious injury. Our technicians use the correct tools and procedures. We carry common spring sizes on our trucks so most Ottawa jobs are completed the same day.

Typical Timeline

From your call to a finished repair: we often schedule same-day or next-day service. On-site, spring replacement usually takes about 45–90 minutes. You’ll get a written quote before any work starts.
